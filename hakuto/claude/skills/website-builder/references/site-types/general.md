# General Site Type

**Reference for website-builder skill. Content generation handled by professional-copywriter skill when needed.**

## Core Characteristics
- **Information-focused**: Build credibility and provide comprehensive company information
- **Company-centric**: Organization, people, and expertise are the hero
- **Relationship-driven**: Goal is inquiry, conversation, consultation—not instant conversion
- **Trust-building**: Establish authority, experience, and reliability

## When to Use This Site Type
- User mentions: Company, organization, agency, studio, firm, business, consultancy
- Content indicates: Services offered, team structure, company history, client work
- Goal: Lead generation, relationship building, consultation requests, RFP responses
- NOT: Software product, SaaS, app with signup flows (use SaaS instead)

## Page Structure Framework

### Homepage
**Purpose**: Establish credibility, provide overview, encourage contact

**Section Framework**:

1. **Hero Section**
   - Company headline (what you do, who you serve)
   - Brief subheadline explaining approach or value
   - Primary CTA: "Get in touch", "Learn more", "Schedule consultation"
   - Hero image (team photo, office, representative imagery—not product screenshot)

2. **What We Do**
   - If services provided → Service overview with brief descriptions
   - If elevator pitch provided → Narrative format explaining purpose
   - If case studies provided → Lead with outcomes, explain approach
   - Adapt based on industry, content depth, style

3. **Why Choose Us / Our Approach**
   - Differentiators, values, or methodology
   - Focus: Trust, expertise, unique approach, track record

4. **About/Team Preview**
   - Brief company history or key people introduction
   - Credentials, awards, years in business
   - Link to full About page

5. **Social Proof** (if provided)
   - Client testimonials with attribution
   - Client logos or partnerships
   - Notable projects or case studies
   - Industry recognition

6. **Contact CTA**
   - Single, clear contact section
   - Less aggressive than SaaS (no urgency)
   - Multiple contact methods if applicable

### About Page
**Purpose**: Build trust through company story, team, credentials

**Structure**:
- Company story: History, founding, evolution
- Mission/values: Why company exists, guiding principles
- Team: Key people with photos, bios, credentials
- Credentials: Awards, certifications, affiliations, recognition
- Location: Office location(s), physical presence

**Adaptations**:
- Compelling founder story → Lead with origin narrative
- Team is strength → Emphasize people and expertise
- Impressive credentials → Highlight early and prominently

### Services Page
**Purpose**: Detail offerings and demonstrate expertise

**Structure**:
- Overview of services offered
- Each service section: Name, description, process/approach, outcomes/deliverables
- Pricing (if transparent) or "Contact for quote"
- CTA at end: "Interested in [service]? Let's talk."

**Adaptations**:
- Process provided → Structure as steps/methodology
- Case studies provided → Include examples under each service
- Packages provided → Show tiered offerings

### Portfolio/Work Page
**Purpose**: Showcase past work and demonstrate capability

**Structure**:
- Grid or list of projects/clients
- Each entry: Project name, brief description, outcome/result
- Visuals if provided (screenshots, photos, before/after)
- Filter by industry, service type, or year if diverse
- CTA: "Want similar results? Contact us."

**Adaptations**:
- Agency → Show design/creative work prominently
- Consultancy → Focus on outcomes and metrics
- Service business → Show client list and testimonials

### Team Page
**Purpose**: Build personal connection and demonstrate expertise

**Structure**:
- Individual bios with photos
- Name, role, credentials, brief background
- Optional: Personal touch (hobbies, fun facts)
- LinkedIn or contact links if appropriate

**Adaptations**:
- Small team (2-5) → Detailed bios with personality
- Large team (10+) → Leadership featured, rest by department
- Solo founder → About page serves as team page

### Contact Page
**Purpose**: Facilitate inquiries and consultations

**Standard Structure**:
- Contact form: Name, email, company (required), service interest (dropdown), message, budget (optional)
- Alternative methods: Email, phone, physical address, office hours
- Optional: Embedded map if local business
- Response expectation: "We'll respond within 24 hours"

## Content Adaptation Rules

### User provides FULL content:
1. Extract company positioning → hero headline
2. Structure services/offerings → Services page sections
3. Position credentials/proof → About page and homepage
4. Structure team information → Team page or About section
5. Maintain informational, consultative tone
6. Suggest missing elements if critical

### User provides PARTIAL content:
1. Use provided content as foundation
2. **Website-builder invokes professional-copywriter** for missing sections
3. Match voice and industry
4. Ask for missing elements: services, team bios, company history

### User provides MINIMAL content:
1. **Website-builder invokes professional-copywriter** for structure
2. Ask clarifying questions: services offered? team? years in business?

## Navigation Pattern

**Primary Navigation**:
- About (company story, mission, values)
- Services (what you offer)
- Work or Portfolio (case studies, past projects)
- Team (key people, if significant)
- Contact (how to reach you)

**Secondary Navigation** (footer):
- Blog/Resources, News/Press, Careers, Privacy Policy, Terms

**Optional Items**:
- Case Studies (if distinct from Portfolio)
- Industries (if serving multiple sectors)
- Process/Approach (if methodology is differentiator)

**Adaptations**:
- Solo consultant → Simplify to About, Services, Work, Contact
- Large firm → Add Industries, Locations, Careers
- Creative agency → Emphasize Work/Portfolio
- B2B services → Add Resources/Insights for thought leadership

## Messaging & Tone Guidance

### Core Principles
- **Trust-building**: Emphasize experience, expertise, track record
- **Relationship-oriented**: "We'd love to hear from you", "Let's discuss your needs"
- **Consultative over pushy**: Offer value, ask questions, invite conversation
- **Professional but approachable**: Warm without being casual (unless industry appropriate)

### Industry-Specific Adaptations

**Consulting/Professional Services**:
- Emphasize: Credentials, methodology, client results
- Tone: Authoritative, thoughtful, strategic

**Creative Agency/Design Studio**:
- Emphasize: Portfolio, creative process, awards
- Tone: Creative, energetic, personality-driven

**B2B Services/Contractors**:
- Emphasize: Reliability, quality, certifications
- Tone: Straightforward, competent, no-nonsense

**Tech Consulting/Dev Agency**:
- Emphasize: Technical expertise, modern stack, efficiency
- Tone: Technical but accessible, knowledgeable

## CTA Strategy

**Primary CTA Types**:
- "Contact us" or "Get in touch"
- "Schedule consultation" or "Book a call"
- "Request proposal" or "Get a quote"
- "Let's talk about your project"

**CTA Placement** (1-2 per page, less aggressive than SaaS):
1. Hero section (primary)
2. After services overview
3. End of page (final CTA)
4. Contact page (form)

**CTA Copy Rules**:
- Softer language: "Let's talk" not "Sign up now"
- Consultation-focused: Emphasize conversation, not instant commitment
- Remove friction: "No obligation", "Free consultation"
- Avoid urgency: No countdown timers, fake scarcity

**Secondary CTAs**:
- "View our work", "Download our guide", "Read case studies", "Meet the team"

## Visual Content Hierarchy

**Priority Order**:
1. **Team/people photos** (highest)
   - Professional headshots, team candids, founder/leadership
   - Conveys: Real people, approachable, trustworthy

2. **Client logos/partnerships**
   - Recognizable brands, industry associations
   - "Trusted by" or "Partners with" sections

3. **Office/location imagery**
   - Physical space, local presence, workshop/collaboration spaces

4. **Portfolio/work samples**
   - Past projects, deliverables, before/after, process photos

5. **Service/process illustrations**
   - Diagrams explaining approach, workflow visualizations, infographics

6. **Product imagery** (lower priority)
   - Only if relevant (e.g., manufacturing, e-commerce)

## Social Proof Patterns

**Types** (in order of effectiveness):
1. **Client testimonials**: Full quote with attribution (name, company, role), specific outcomes, video if available
2. **Client list/logos**: Recognizable brands, "Trusted by" sections, industry diversity
3. **Case studies**: Full stories with context, challenge, solution, results, metrics
4. **Years in business**: "Serving [area] since [year]", track record = trust
5. **Team credentials**: Degrees, certifications, awards, publications, speaking engagements
6. **Industry affiliations**: Chamber, professional associations, BBB, certifications
7. **Press mentions/awards**: Featured in [Publication], Winner of [Award]

**Positioning**:
- Homepage: After "What We Do", before Contact
- About page: Credentials and awards prominently
- Services page: Testimonials specific to each service

**If none provided**: Create placeholder sections, suggest adding, highlight team credentials or years in business

## Trust Signals

**Essential**:
- Physical location/address: Shows you're real, local
- Team bios with credentials: Real people with expertise
- Years in business: "Established 2005", "15 years serving [area]"
- Industry affiliations: Member of [Association], [Certification]
- Client list/logos: Social proof through brand recognition
- Response time commitment: "We respond within 24 hours"
- Privacy/security: Clear privacy policy, secure contact forms

**Positioning**:
- Footer: Address, phone, email, industry badges
- About page: History, credentials, affiliations
- Contact page: Multiple methods, response commitment
- Homepage: Years in business, client logos

## Technical Considerations

**Performance**: Portfolio images optimized, lazy loading, team photos consistent size

**Forms**: Contact form with validation, longer forms acceptable, fields for context, spam protection, thank you page

**SEO**:
- Title: [Company Name] - [Services] in [Location] (if local)
- Meta: Services, target clients, location if relevant
- Schema: LocalBusiness/Organization, Service, ContactPoint
- Location pages if multi-location

**Maps**: Embedded Google Maps if physical location matters (local businesses, office visits)

**Analytics**: Track contact form submissions, phone/email clicks, time on About page, less conversion-focused (relationship metrics)

## Common Page Patterns

**Process/Methodology**: Step-by-step approach, visual workflow, what client expects at each stage

**Industries Served**: If multiple sectors, industry-specific challenges/solutions, case studies per industry

**Resources/Blog**: See `features/blog-section.md` - General blogs focus on thought leadership, industry insights, company news, how-to guides

**FAQ**: Common questions about services, pricing, process, reduces contact friction

**Careers**: If hiring priority, open positions, company culture/values, application process

**News/Press**: Company announcements, press mentions, awards, speaking engagements
